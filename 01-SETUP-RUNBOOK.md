# 01 · Shopify Setup Runbook (Canada)

Follow these in order. Total time to a working store: a focused weekend. Each step says exactly where to click.

---

## Step 1 — Domain & account (30 min)

1. Go to **shopify.com** and start the free trial (no card needed to build; you only pay when you launch).
2. When asked, set:
   - **Store name:** Card Vault Customs
   - **Currency:** CAD (Settings → General → Store currency — set this *before* adding products, it's hard to change later)
   - **Address/region:** your Canadian address
3. Buy your domain. Two options:
   - **Easiest:** buy it through Shopify (Settings → Domains → Buy new domain) — auto-connects, ~$20 CAD/yr.
   - **Cheaper:** buy at Namecheap/Cloudflare and connect it (Settings → Domains → Connect existing).
   - Target: `cardvaultcustoms.com` (and grab `.ca` too if available so no one else takes it).

> Pick a plan later: **Shopify Basic** (~$51 CAD/mo, or ~$38/mo billed yearly) is all you need to start.

---

## Step 2 — Theme (20 min)

1. **Online Store → Themes**. The default **Dawn** theme is free, fast, and clean — use it.
2. Click **Customize** to open the editor. You'll come back here in Step 6.
3. Don't buy a paid theme yet. Dawn is more than enough to launch and look professional.

---

## Step 3 — Products (1–2 hrs)

1. Open **`02-PRODUCTS.md`** and create each product under **Products → Add product**.
2. For each: paste the **title**, paste the **description** into the description box, set the **price** (CAD), and upload 3–5 photos of your actual slabs.
3. Set **Inventory → "Track quantity" OFF** (or to a high number) since these are made-to-order, so you never go "out of stock."
4. Create **Collections** (Products → Collections): "Pokémon", "One Piece", "Quick Buy / Seasonal".

---

## Step 4 — Photo uploads (the key step for custom cards) (45 min)

Your customers must send you the image they want on their card. Shopify's cart can't collect files alone, so add an app:

1. **Apps → Shopify App Store**, search **"product options file upload"**.
2. Recommended free/low-cost options: **Easify Product Options**, **Hulk Product Options**, or **Avis File Upload**.
3. Install one, then add these fields to each custom product:
   - **Upload your image(s)** (file upload, required) — allow JPG/PNG/HEIC, up to ~20 MB.
   - **Card name / text on slab** (text box).
   - **Pokémon or One Piece?** (dropdown).
   - **Card finish** (dropdown: Holographic / Foil / Matte / Glossy).
   - **Border style** (dropdown: Classic / Gold / Silver / Black / Full-art borderless).
   - **Grade number to display** (dropdown 1–10, default 10 — purely cosmetic).
   - **Notes for the design** (text box, optional).
   - **Preferred proof method** (dropdown: Text / Email).
   - **Mobile number for design approval** (text box) — so you can text the proof.
   - **Email for design approval** (text box) — so you can email the proof and keep a written approval record.

---

## Step 5 — Payments (20 min)

1. **Settings → Payments → Activate Shopify Payments.**
2. You'll enter business details + a Canadian bank account for payouts. Sole proprietorship is fine to start — you don't need an incorporated business.
3. This automatically enables **Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Apple Pay, Google Pay**.
4. Turn on **Shop Pay** and (optionally) **PayPal** in the same screen for more checkout options.
5. Fees on Basic: ~2.9% + $0.30 per online transaction. No extra Shopify fee when using Shopify Payments.

> **Taxes:** In Settings → Taxes, Shopify auto-handles Canadian GST/HST/PST by province once you add your tax numbers. If you're under the $30k/yr small-supplier threshold you may not need to register yet — confirm with an accountant, but you can launch first and add tax numbers later.

---

## Step 6 — Shipping (45 min)

1. **Settings → Shipping and delivery.**
2. Create a shipping zone for **Canada**:
   - Offer a flat rate (e.g. **$5–8 CAD** for a bubble mailer / small box).
   - Add a **"Free shipping over $X"** tier (e.g. free over $50) — great for boosting order size, like RC Custom Cards' "free over $30".
3. Create a zone for **United States** and **International** if you'll ship abroad (slabs are light, so this is easy money). Set higher flat rates (e.g. $15 US / $25 intl) or connect **Canada Post calculated rates**.
4. Buy a cheap **digital kitchen scale** and standardize on one mailer size so your rates stay accurate.
5. Print labels right from Shopify (**Orders → buy shipping label**) — discounted Canada Post rates included.

---

## Step 7 — Pages & policies (45 min)

1. **Online Store → Pages → Add page** for each section in **`03-PAGES.md`** (About, How It Works, FAQ).
2. **Settings → Policies** — paste each policy from **`04-POLICIES.md`** (Refund, Shipping, Privacy, Terms). Shopify links these in the footer automatically.
3. Add the **custom-novelty disclaimer** (from `04-POLICIES.md`) to: every product description, the FAQ, and the footer. This matters.
4. Set up your **homepage** sections in the theme editor using the copy in `03-PAGES.md` (hero banner, How It Works 3-step, featured products, reviews).

---

## Step 8 — Connect Instagram (30 min)

1. **Settings → Apps and sales channels → add the Facebook & Instagram channel.**
2. Link your `@cardvaultcustoms` account (you'll need it connected to a Facebook business page — the setup wizard walks you through it).
3. Once approved, you can **tag products in posts/stories** and add the **"View Shop"** button. Put your store link in your IG bio immediately.

---

## Step 9 — Test, then launch (30 min)

1. Place a **real test order** on yourself using **Settings → Payments → test mode** (or a real $1 product you refund). Confirm: photo upload arrives, confirmation email sends, you can print a label.
2. **Online Store → Preferences → remove password protection** to go live.
3. Tell your IG followers, run a launch promo (copy a proven one: **"Buy 2 Get 1 Free — code BUY2GET1"**).

---

## Ongoing weekly rhythm

- Check **Orders** daily; text design proofs within 48h (set this expectation on the site).
- Once approved, print + ship within 48h.
- Re-share customer slabs to your IG — social proof sells these.
